# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business and stakeholder needs into clear, testable requirements and process changes that delivery teams can execute.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate requirement discovery and clarify scope boundaries
- Document business rules, assumptions, and non-functional needs
- Partner with PdM and PM to refine backlog items before planning
- Support acceptance criteria quality and requirement traceability

### Goals
- Reduce requirement ambiguity and rework
- Ensure business intent is preserved through delivery
- Improve handoffs from discovery to implementation

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Backlog refinement notes and open-question logs
- Clarification responses during planning and execution

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure solutions are usable and aligned to user needs by leading interaction design, content flows, and usability validation.

### Responsibilities
- Produce user flows, wireframes, and interaction guidance
- Partner with PdM on user problem framing and success signals
- Collaborate with developers to ensure feasible implementation
- Participate in demo/release validation for usability concerns

### Goals
- Improve user success and reduce usability friction
- Surface design risks early before development lock-in
- Align delivered experience with product intent

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and async design comments
- Handoff notes for engineering implementation
- Usability findings shared with PdM/PM and stakeholders

---

## QA / Test Leads

### Role Summary
QA / Test Leads define and coordinate quality strategy across test levels, making release readiness and defect risk visible.

### Responsibilities
- Define test approach for unit, integration, and end-to-end coverage
- Validate acceptance criteria and release readiness status
- Track defect trends and coordinate remediation priorities
- Maintain quality entry/exit criteria for release gates

### Goals
- Prevent escaped defects on critical workflows
- Improve confidence in release quality
- Shorten validation feedback loops

### Typical Communication
- Test plans, quality dashboards, and defect triage updates
- Release readiness sign-off notes
- Risk escalation when quality thresholds are missed

---

## Scrum Masters / Delivery Leads

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters / Delivery Leads improve execution flow by facilitating ceremonies, removing blockers, and coaching teams on consistent delivery practices.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify flow bottlenecks and unblock cross-team dependencies
- Partner with PM on delivery risks and schedule pressure
- Reinforce agreed team working agreements and cadences

### Goals
- Increase predictability and delivery throughput
- Reduce blockers and coordination delays
- Maintain healthy, sustainable team execution

### Typical Communication
- Ceremony facilitation and follow-up action summaries
- Blocker and dependency escalations
- Iteration health checks shared with PM/PdM

---

## Stakeholder Champions

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Champions represent a stakeholder domain (for example Support, Sales, Compliance, or Operations) and ensure domain impacts are visible in decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Bring domain-specific requirements and constraints into planning
- Review milestones and communicate downstream readiness needs
- Escalate business risks when trade-offs impact their domain
- Confirm launch communications and enablement are complete

### Goals
- Prevent missed stakeholder impacts at release time
- Improve decision quality with domain context
- Strengthen trust through predictable communication

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and readiness checkpoints
- Business impact feedback during risk and release discussions
- Adoption/readiness updates after launch

---

## Collaboration and Handoff Expectations

- Every phase should identify one accountable owner and named responsible contributors.
- Requirement handoff: PdM/BA -> Developers/QA includes acceptance criteria and dependencies.
- Design handoff: UX -> Developers/QA includes flows, edge cases, and success states.
- Execution handoff: Developers -> QA includes test evidence and known limitations.
- Release handoff: PM/QA -> Stakeholders includes readiness status, rollout plan, and support notes.
- Retrospective handoff: PM/Scrum Master -> all owners includes action items with due dates.
